Field note — why this trick runs the world

The slider is the whole lesson. Each circle is one pure frequency: the big slow ones carry the overall shape, the small fast ones carry the detail, and stacking more of them sharpens the copy. That is a Fourier series — no equation required. This same trick is how JPEG stores photos (keep the big circles, throw away the tiny ones), how your phone isolates your voice from street noise, and how audio software pulls a single instrument out of a mix.
